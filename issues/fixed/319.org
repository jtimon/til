** FIXED #319 Reject raw allocations into Dynamic locals

Post-mortem:

The typer treated malloc / calloc / realloc like ordinary Dynamic-returning expressions at declaration sites. That let raw allocation results initialize borrowed ref and Dynamic locals, even though a concrete local is the source-level raw pointer spelling.

The fix converts user-facing Dynamic allocation declarations to concrete raw pointer locals and rejects raw allocation declarations into borrowed ref or Dynamic locals. Concrete locals are already owned by default. Compiler-internal src/self ABI slots remain erased Dynamic pointers because write_ptr borrows and their ownership moves through raw runtime storage. The regression cases are bad_raw_alloc_ref and bad_raw_alloc_dynamic in test/typer_errors.til.

This used to compile:

#+begin_src til
p : Dynamic = malloc(8)
free(own p)
#+end_src

Raw allocation ownership should use a concrete raw byte pointer, e.g. U8, or a container such as Vec / DynVec that owns its backing buffer.
