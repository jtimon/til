** FIXED #305 Accessor inline (#300) missed hoisted bodies: unsafe_get emitted 18M real calls

Fixed here (created directly in fixed/): the generalized len/unsafe_get
accessor inline landed in #300, but the --prof snapshot refreshed after the
core_func conversions showed Vec__Str_unsafe_get with 18.1M REAL calls and
Vec__Expr_unsafe_get with 11.1M per self-host translate -- while `len` inlined
fine at the very same call sites (the same for-in loop emitted
`_fc->count` inline and then a real `Vec__Str_unsafe_get(_fc, &_fi)` call
three lines later).

Root cause, two layers. (1) #300's inlinability check required the callee
body to be a single `return EXPR` statement. That check runs at emit time,
but the desugarer's hoisting pass has already rewritten any NESTED body by
then -- Vec.unsafe_get's

    return ptr_add(self.data, i.mul(T.size()).cast(U64))

becomes a chain of four single-init `hoisted__*` Decls feeding a trivial
`return hoisted__v_N`. Four statements, not one, so the gate said no and
every unsafe_get call stayed a real call. `len`'s `return self.count` has
nothing to hoist, which is why it kept inlining and masked the miss.
(2) The garbager then appends cleanup calls (`hoisted__X.delete(...)`) for
the scalar temps -- no-ops at emit time (value scalars have no heap to
free), but real AST statements that both break any "Decls then Return"
shape check and make each cleaned-up temp count TWO uses (its real use
plus the delete arg).

Fix (src/self/builder.til): teach the inline to see through both layers.

- builder_is_temp_delete recognizes the garbager's scalar-temp cleanup
  shape (an FCall to `.delete` whose arg is a `hoisted__*` Ident of
  primitive type).
- builder_is_inline_accessor now also accepts a body of N `hoisted__*`
  Decls (each with exactly one init, each used exactly once outside the
  delete cleanups) followed by the Return -- the exact shape
  hoist_to_temp + garbager produce. The use-exactly-once requirement
  keeps the splice from duplicating or dropping a temp's computation.
- emit_inline_accessor splices each temp's init back into its single use,
  oldest first (so a later init's reference to an earlier temp is expanded
  by the time its own turn comes), skipping the delete cleanups on both
  sides (they are never emitted, and substituting into one would rewrite
  the hoisted__ Ident arg that identifies it). That inverse-hoisting
  reconstructs the pristine pre-hoist expression, and the existing
  single-expression splice machinery (param substitution + ref/value
  return emit) handles the rest unchanged. A body with no temps
  degenerates to the old path.

Regression evidence: in the regenerated compiler, Vec__Str_unsafe_get /
Vec__Expr_unsafe_get vanish from the --prof top-32 (the refreshed tables in
doc/profiling.org are the record); the remaining Vec__*_unsafe_get(
references in boot/til.c are declarations and dyn-dispatch wrappers, not
calls. make test_two_pass stays green.
