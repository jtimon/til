** OPEN #124 Remove remaining manual frees for typed raw-buffer locals

Status update (2026-07-18): the original premise is obsolete. ~Dynamic~ never
owns; it is for type erasure and raw borrows. Issue #302 established the
replacement model, and #319 rejects user-facing allocator results bound to
~Dynamic~ or ~ref~ locals.

Allocator-rooted concrete locals such as:

#+begin_src til
buf : U8 = malloc(n)
#+end_src

are ordinary owned locals. Raw-allocation provenance makes them pointer-form
heap blocks, and the garbager inserts their plain ~free~ at last use and exit
edges. Source-level ~own~ is redundant on locals, so this issue does not
propose an owned-Dynamic rule, a new raw-buffer type, or an ~own U8~ local
spelling.

The remaining cleanup is narrower:

- Remove handwritten ~free(own buf)~ calls where ~buf~ is a concrete
  allocator-rooted local and automatic cleanup or an existing ownership
  transfer already handles it.
- Keep explicit frees that are real behavior: custom/manual delete
  implementations, foreign ownership, custom allocators, and buffers that
  intentionally do not use plain ~free~.
- Keep compiler-internal ~src/self~ erased ABI slots until #286 gives their
  ~write_ptr~ / interpreter heap transfers typed ownership. Those slots are
  not evidence that ordinary ~Dynamic~ values may own memory.
- Update ~qualifiers.org~ and ~c2til.org~ so they describe concrete typed raw
  locals and remove the old manual-free contract.

Issue #302 remains the ownership-pass source of truth. This issue is the
focused explicit-call and documentation cleanup after that machinery lands.

Acceptance:

- Census remaining handwritten frees of allocator-rooted locals and classify
  every survivor as required behavior or remove it.
- Ordinary user code contains no owned ~Dynamic~ allocation convention.
- Typed raw locals need no handwritten cleanup on normal or early-exit paths.
- The full ASAN suite passes after the removals.
